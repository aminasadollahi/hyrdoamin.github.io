Microplastic Research

This project focuses on the assessment and physicochemical degradation of microplastic fibers released from synthetic fabrics in aquatic environments. The study investigates how environmental conditions, particularly pH and UV exposure, influence the degradation behavior of both natural and synthetic fibers. Laboratory analyses, including FTIR and SEM, are used to evaluate chemical and physical changes, while measurements of organic carbon and mass loss provide insight into degradation rates. The results contribute to a better understanding of microplastic fate, transport, and potential environmental impacts in water systems.

Predicting Flow Rate Using Machine Learning

This project focuses on predicting hydrologic flow rates using machine learning models. Specifically, Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and Support Vector Machines (SVM) were developed and trained on historical flow data. The study compares the performance of these models in terms of accuracy, generalization, and robustness, providing insights into the most effective approach for data-driven flow prediction. The results demonstrate the potential of machine learning techniques to improve water resources modeling and management.
